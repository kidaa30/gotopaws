Bandit

Age: 1 year, 1 month

Size: Medium

Breed: Boxer Mix

Shelter: Austin Pets Alive

City: Austin

Bio:

Bandit is a Velcro dog. He wants to stick to his beloved human at all times.

This sweet boy is a snugglebug who never wants to leave his human's side, even in the car. At home, he wants to cuddle and give kisses.

He does relate better to female humans, it should be said. On first meeting men, he can be skittish and standoffish, but this should pass when he gets well acquainted with them.

Bandit is in foster care where he's in a little competition with the foster mom's dog who is equally possessive of her attention. He's only been observed looking with curiosity at cats, but not interacting, and so far has not been seen with kids. He's a friendly boy, though, who should get along well with older kids.

He's sleeping well in his crate and seems house trained. On his leash, he walks well, but pulls some, and he listens well, answering to the Sit and Shake commands.

Bandit is an active boy who likes to jump around and tends to get mouthy when excited, although he does no harm and stops when told No. He drags one leg some because he has an injury that hasn't entirely healed, but it doesn't slow him down.

Bandit only asks for a forever home where he can be in the company of people he can adore. He may do best as the only dog or with a submissive dog who won't be as possessive of the family humans. Come visit and see if he's right for your home.
